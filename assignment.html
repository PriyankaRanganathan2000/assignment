Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka, historically known as Ceylon is an island in South Asia. It lies in the Indian Ocean, southwest of the Bay of Bengal, separated from the Indian peninsula by the Gulf of Mannar and the Palk Strait. It shares a maritime border with the Maldives in the southwest and India in the northwest.Sri Lanka has a population of approximately 22 million and is home to many cultures, languages and ethnicities.There are 4 main religions.

  • Buddhism
  • Hinduism
  • Islam
  • Christianity
And 3 languages
  1. Sinhala
  2. Tamil
  3. English (Recognised language)
Area: 65,610.2 km2
Anthem: Sri Lanka Matha

National flag

When Sri Lanka gained her independence from Great Britain , it was the lion flag of the last king of Sri Lanka was hoisted once again. The first Prime Minister of independent Sri Lanka, D.S. Senanayake, appointed a committee to advice the government on the design of a new national flag.

Symbolism

Symbol Meaning
The Lion The Sinhala ethnicity and the strength of the nation
The bo leaves The four Buddhist virtues of loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy and equanimit
The sword of the lion The sovereignty of the nation
Orange stripe The Tamil ethnicity
Green stripe The Muslim ethnicity
The maroon background The Sinhala ethnicity
The golden yellow border Other minority communities of Sri Lanka
